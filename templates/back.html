Back Training

BUILD A STRONG AND POWERFUL BACK

The back is one of the most important muscle groups for posture, strength, and overall physique. Back exercises target muscles such as the latissimus dorsi, trapezius, rhomboids, rear deltoids, and erector spinae. Compound movements like pull-ups, barbell rows, and deadlifts develop thickness and strength, while isolation exercises like face pulls, cable rows, and chest-supported incline rows focus on definition and symmetry. Incorporating a mix of free weights, machines, cables, and bodyweight exercises ensures full back development, enhances posture, and reduces the risk of injury.

Pull-Ups

Bodyweight exercise targeting lats, biceps, and shoulders for width.

Lat Pulldown

Great for beginners, targets lats and biceps with controlled motion.

Barbell Bent-Over Row

Builds middle back thickness and engages traps and rhomboids.

Dumbbell Single-Arm Row

Targets each side individually for balanced back development.

T-Bar Row

Builds thickness in middle back and improves grip strength.

Seated Cable Row

Maintains constant tension while strengthening lats, traps, and rhomboids.

Face Pulls

Strengthens rear delts, traps, and improves posture.

Deadlift

Full posterior chain exercise, strengthens lower back, traps, and glutes.

Hyperextensions (Back Extensions)

Targets lower back and glutes, improving posture and core stability.

Chest Supported Incline Row

Isolation row that focuses on mid-back thickness without straining the lower back.
